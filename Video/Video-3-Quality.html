Video Quality

Here are some results from running on the sintel animated clip.  All results are in kbps (thousand bits per second).  The clip was built from lossless .png files of each frame.  The video is 1920x854 (we'll call this 1080p even though it is not) and 960x428 (we'll call this 480p even though it is not).  The 480p video has 1/4 the size (pixels) of the 1080p video.  These values were all determined by watching the videos output with them (and many other candidates), to try to determine what met the various levels of quality. Because of this, the results are subjective, but should give you a good feeling for the power of the various codecs, when used with the default settings.  raw video, ffv1, mpeg4, mjpeg, and mpeg2 were all encoded with the encoders inside the ffmpeg too.  The other encoders that were used are: libx264 (with various pre-set configurations), libvpx, and libtheora.  The "Almost Perfect" settings were determined by looking at single frames (available on the pages for the individual runs by clicking the column header) and seeing *almost* no visible degradation.  The "Good" settings were determined by watching the clips at full speed and not being able to see any substantial artifacts (blur, macro-blocks, etc).
